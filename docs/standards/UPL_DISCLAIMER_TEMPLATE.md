## Purpose

This template provides standard attorney supervision and Unauthorized Practice of Law (UPL) boundary language for all Glass Box products used by or marketed to California WC attorneys. Every product's T1 and T2 documentation must include the applicable statements from this template, verbatim.

UPL risk arises when an AI tool's output is used in legal proceedings without adequate attorney review, or when the tool's framing implies that outputs are ready for use without attorney verification. California Business and Professions Code §6125 prohibits the practice of law by unlicensed persons. Attorney-facing AI tools that obscure this boundary create liability for the attorney and reputational risk for Glass Box.

---

## Required Verbatim UPL Statement

The following statement is **required in all T1 and T2 documentation** for every Glass Box product that produces outputs used in connection with legal matters. It must appear verbatim. Paraphrasing or abbreviation is not permitted.

> **This tool assists attorney review; it does not provide legal advice. All outputs require attorney supervision and verification before use in legal proceedings.**

**Placement:** This statement must appear:
- In the product overview section of all T1 documentation
- At the beginning of any section describing AI-generated output features
- In the paralegal operating guide (T2 documentation) before the first description of output use

>

## "Not Legal Advice" Variants by Feature Type

### Document Classification Output

> This document classification is produced by an AI model and is presented for attorney review. It is not a legal determination. The supervising attorney must verify the classification before relying on it for any legal purpose.

### Extracted Field Values (form data, medical record parsing)

> These field values were extracted by AI from the uploaded document. AI extraction may contain errors, particularly in handwritten documents, low-quality scans, or documents with non-standard formatting. The supervising attorney or paralegal must verify all extracted values against the source document before use.

### Legal Research Output (case citations, statutory references)

> This legal research is generated by AI and is provided for attorney review only. Citations must be independently verified for accuracy, current validity, and applicability to the specific matter before use in any filing or legal proceeding. AI-generated legal research may contain hallucinated citations or misstatements of law.

### Permanent Disability Rating Calculation

> This PD rating calculation is generated using the PDRS 2005 and AMA Guides 5th Edition inputs provided by the user. The calculation is produced for attorney review and does not constitute an official rating determination. All input values must be verified by the supervising attorney. Final PD ratings must be determined through the DWC rating process.

### Case Summary or Chronology

> This case summary was generated by AI from the documents uploaded to this matter. It may omit relevant information, mischaracterize facts, or reflect document extraction errors. The supervising attorney must review the summary against underlying source documents before relying on it for any legal purpose.

## Attorney Verification Checklist Template

Include this checklist in T1 and T2 documentation for any feature that produces content used in legal proceedings. Adapt the specific items to the feature described.

The supervising attorney must verify the following before relying on AI output:

**Factual Accuracy**
- [ ] Claimant name and claim number match the matter on file
- [ ] All dates (date of injury, treatment dates, form deadlines) are accurate
- [ ] All diagnosis codes (ICD-10) are correctly transcribed from source medical records
- [ ] All medical provider names and license numbers are correctly identified
- [ ] All dollar amounts (medical costs, indemnity, PD ratings) are correctly calculated

**Legal Accuracy**
- [ ] Applicable statutes and regulations cited are current and correctly stated
- [ ] Case citations (if any) have been independently verified in Westlaw, Lexis, or CourtListener
- [ ] PD rating inputs (WPI, apportionment, occupation, age) reflect the attorney's independent analysis
- [ ] Form fields comply with current DWC form requirements (verify form version date)

**Procedural Compliance**
- [ ] Filing deadlines are correctly identified
- [ ] Jurisdiction and venue are correctly identified
- [ ] Service requirements have been confirmed
- [ ] Court-specific formatting rules are satisfied

**Privilege and Confidentiality**
- [ ] No attorney-client privileged communications appear in outputs shared outside the firm
- [ ] No PHI appears in outputs sent to parties who are not authorized to receive it

## Escalation Trigger List

The following conditions require the paralegal to stop using the tool and escalate to the supervising attorney before proceeding:

1. **Conflicting data:** The tool produces output that conflicts with a known fact in the case file (e.g., different date of injury than the DWC-1 form on file).

2. **Low-confidence flag:** The tool displays a confidence warning or flags a field as uncertain or unverifiable.

3. **Missing source document:** The tool produces output that references a document that is not in the uploaded matter file.

4. **Unexpected classification:** The tool classifies a document differently than the paralegal would expect given the document's content.

5. **Tool unavailability during deadline:** The tool is unavailable and a filing deadline is within 24 hours. Follow the manual backup procedure and notify the supervising attorney immediately.

6. **Error message during processing:** The tool returns an error during document processing. Do not use partial outputs. Report the error per the Error Reporting Procedure below and await guidance.

7. **PHI of a non-party appears in output:** The tool surfaces PHI of a person who is not the claimant in the active matter.

8. **Output contradicts supervising attorney's instruction:** The tool produces output that conflicts with a specific instruction given by the supervising attorney for this matter.

## Error Reporting Procedure Template

Use this template to report AI errors to Glass Box and to document the error in the matter file.

**Step 1: Stop using the affected output.** Do not submit, file, or share AI output that appears to contain an error.

**Step 2: Document the error in the matter file.**
- Date and time of error
- Feature used (document classification, extracted fields, PD calculation, etc.)
- Description of the error (what the tool produced vs. what was expected)
- Screenshot or export of the erroneous output (if possible)
- Name of the supervising attorney notified

**Step 3: Notify the supervising attorney.** Present the documented error and the screenshot. The attorney decides whether to:
- Correct the output manually and proceed
- Decline to use the AI output for this task
- Report the error to Glass Box

**Step 4: Report to Glass Box** (if the supervising attorney directs).
- **Email:** [product-specific error reporting alias — populate before publication]
- **Subject:** AI Error Report — [Product Name] — [Matter ID]
- **Include:** Description of error, feature used, date/time, screenshot or export

**Step 5: Receive acknowledgment.** Glass Box will acknowledge error reports within one business day. If the error indicates a systemic issue, Glass Box will notify all affected customers.

## UPL Risk Situations — Reference for T1 Documentation

The following situations represent the highest UPL risk in AI-assisted WC practice. T1 documentation must address each of these explicitly when the product feature is applicable.

| Situation | UPL Risk | Required Control |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| AI generates a form pre-populated with claimant data | High — form may be filed without attorney review | Mandatory attorney approval step in workflow before form is exportable |
| AI generates a demand letter or position statement | High — document resembles legal advice | Document must be labeled "Draft — Attorney Review Required" and cannot be sent directly from the tool |
| AI produces a PD rating figure | Medium-High — figure may be accepted without verification | Rating must be labeled "Preliminary — Requires Attorney Verification" and audit log must record the supervising attorney who approved |
| AI retrieves and summarizes case law | Medium — citation may be hallucinated | All citations must include a "Verify citation" prompt before export; tool must not represent citations as verified |
| AI classifies a document as privileged or non-privileged | Medium — incorrect classification could waive privilege | Classification must be labeled "Suggested Classification — Attorney Review Required" |
| AI produces a chronology for use in deposition preparation | Low-Medium — errors in chronology could affect case strategy | Chronology must be labeled "AI-Generated Draft" and verified against source documents before use |
