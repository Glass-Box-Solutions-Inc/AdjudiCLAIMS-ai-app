## Purpose

This template provides standard UPL boundary language for all Glass Box products used by or marketed to California WC claims examiners and adjusters. AdjudiCLAIMS operates under a fundamentally different UPL risk profile than the attorney product:

- **Attorney product:** The user IS a licensed professional. AI assists the practice of law. UPL risk is limited to unsupervised paralegal use.
- **AdjudiCLAIMS:** The user is NOT a licensed attorney. **Any AI output constituting legal advice, legal analysis, or legal conclusions is unauthorized practice of law under Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 6125.** UPL risk is present in EVERY feature that touches legal subject matter.

This template implements the **Green/Yellow/Red zone framework** defined in [WC_CLAIMS_EXAMINER_ROLES_AND_DUTIES.md Part 3.5](../product/foundations/WC_CLAIMS_EXAMINER_ROLES_AND_DUTIES.md). Every AI output in AdjudiCLAIMS must be classified into a zone, and the corresponding disclaimer language from this template must be applied. **Verbatim use is required. Paraphrasing or abbreviation is not permitted.**

## Required Verbatim Product Disclaimer

The following statement is **required on every screen, every report, and every output** of AdjudiCLAIMS. It must appear verbatim. It replaces the attorney product's "assists attorney review" statement.

> **This tool provides factual information and data analysis to support claims management decisions. It does not provide legal advice, legal analysis, or legal conclusions. All substantive claims decisions must be made by the claims examiner using independent professional judgment. When legal issues are involved, consult your assigned defense counsel or in-house legal department.**

**Placement:** This statement must appear:
- On the product login/home screen
- In the header or footer of every AI-generated output
- At the beginning of every chat session
- On every exported document or report
- In all product documentation, training materials, and marketing collateral

## Zone-Specific Disclaimer Language

### GREEN ZONE — Factual/Arithmetic Outputs

**When to use:** Outputs that present extracted facts, statutory calculations, regulatory deadlines, document summaries, or guideline matching. No legal analysis involved.

**Required disclaimer (brief):**

> **AI-generated factual summary. Verify against source documents.**

**Required disclaimer (extended — use for medical record summaries, benefit calculations, and deadline tracking):**

> This information was extracted by AI from uploaded claim documents. It presents factual data only and does not constitute a claims determination, legal analysis, or recommendation. Verify all facts, dates, and calculations against source documents before relying on this output for any claims decision.

**GREEN zone feature-specific variants:**

#### Medical Record Summary

> This medical record summary was generated by AI from the documents in this claim file. It presents extracted factual findings (diagnoses, WPI ratings, work restrictions, treatment recommendations) as stated in the source documents. It does not interpret medical findings or assess their legal significance. Verify all extracted data against the source medical report before use.

#### Benefit Calculation (TD Rate, PD Advance)

> This benefit calculation applies the statutory formula to the data provided. It is arithmetic only and does not constitute a benefits determination. Verify all input values (average weekly earnings, date of injury, statutory minimum/maximum rates) before relying on this calculation. The claims examiner makes all benefits determinations.

#### Regulatory Deadline Tracking

> These deadlines are calculated from statutory requirements (Labor Code, CCR Title 8, CCR Title 10) applied to the dates in the claim file. Verify that the underlying dates (date of claim receipt, date of employer knowledge, date of proof of loss) are accurate. Deadline calculations are procedural — consult your compliance department or legal counsel if you have questions about how a deadline applies to a specific claim situation.

#### Document Classification

> This document was classified by AI based on its content. AI classification may contain errors. Review the classification and correct it if inaccurate. Document classification is organizational — it does not constitute a determination of the document's legal significance.

#### Claim Chronology / Timeline

> This timeline was generated by AI from the documents in this claim file. It presents dates and events as extracted from source documents. It may omit events, contain incorrect dates, or mischaracterize document content. Review against source documents before relying on this timeline for any claims decision.

### YELLOW ZONE — Outputs With Legal Implications

**When to use:** Outputs that identify potential legal issues, present comparable claims data, flag medical evidence inconsistencies, or provide information that could inform legal analysis — without itself constituting legal analysis.

**Required disclaimer (mandatory — must appear with every YELLOW zone output):**

> **⚠️ This information may involve legal issues. Consult with assigned defense counsel or in-house legal before making decisions based on this information.**

**Required disclaimer (extended — use for comparable claims data, litigation risk indicators, and issue identification):**

> This output presents factual information and statistical data that may have legal implications for this claim. It is provided for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal analysis, legal advice, or a recommendation regarding any legal issue. Before making claims decisions based on this information, consult with your assigned defense counsel or in-house legal department.

**YELLOW zone feature-specific variants:**

#### Comparable Claims Data / Resolution Ranges

> The comparable claims data presented below reflects historical outcomes for claims with similar characteristics. These figures are statistical — they do not represent an evaluation of this claim's value, a settlement recommendation, or a legal analysis of case strength. Historical outcomes may not predict future results. **Consult defense counsel for case valuation and settlement strategy.**

#### Litigation Risk Indicators

> This claim has been flagged with characteristics statistically associated with higher litigation rates. This is a data-driven observation based on claim characteristics, not a legal assessment of this claim. The presence of these indicators does not mean litigation will occur, nor does it constitute a recommendation to refer to counsel. **The decision to refer to defense counsel is yours. If you determine referral is appropriate, consult defense counsel for legal analysis.**

#### Medical Evidence Inconsistency Flag

> AI has identified a discrepancy between medical reports in this claim file (details below). This flag presents a factual observation — it does not assess the legal significance of the discrepancy or recommend a course of action. **Medical evidence inconsistencies may be relevant to case evaluation. Consult defense counsel regarding the implications for this claim.**

#### Subrogation / Third-Party Indicator

> AI has identified information in the claim file suggesting potential third-party involvement. Third-party involvement may implicate subrogation rights and require legal analysis. **This is a factual observation only. Consult defense counsel for subrogation analysis and legal advice.**

#### Cumulative Trauma / Multi-Employer Exposure

> This claim involves characteristics that may raise cumulative trauma and/or multi-employer exposure issues. These are legal questions that require analysis by a licensed attorney. **This is an issue identification, not a legal analysis. Consult defense counsel regarding employer liability, apportionment, and related legal issues.**

#### Reserve Adequacy Analysis

> The reserve analysis below presents comparable claims data and exposure components for your review. It is factual and statistical information — not a reserve recommendation or a legal assessment of claim value. Reserve decisions require your independent professional judgment. **If reserve adjustments may be influenced by legal issues (coverage disputes, apportionment, settlement strategy), consult defense counsel before adjusting.**

### RED ZONE — Blocked Outputs (Mandatory Attorney Referral)

**When to use:** The AI has detected that a query or output would constitute legal advice, legal analysis, legal conclusions, case law interpretation, coverage opinions, settlement strategy, or any other output that crosses the UPL boundary. The output is BLOCKED entirely.

**Required referral message (mandatory — replaces the blocked output):**

> **🛑 This question involves a legal issue that requires analysis by a licensed attorney.**
>
> Contact your assigned defense counsel or in-house legal department for guidance on this matter.
>
> I can help you prepare a **factual claim summary** for your counsel referral that includes the relevant medical evidence, claim data, and timeline. Would you like me to generate one?

**RED zone trigger-specific variants:**

#### Query Requesting Legal Advice ("Should I deny this claim?")

> **🛑 Coverage determinations involving disputed facts or legal questions require legal analysis.**
>
> I can present the factual basis of this claim — the medical evidence, employment data, and policy provisions — but whether to accept or deny requires your independent judgment, and if legal issues are involved, guidance from defense counsel.
>
> Would you like me to generate a factual claim summary for counsel referral?

#### Query Requesting Case Evaluation ("Is this a strong claim?")

> **🛑 Evaluating the legal strength of a claim requires analysis by a licensed attorney.**
>
> I can present the medical evidence, comparable claims data, and claim characteristics, but assessing case strength is a legal determination that must be made by defense counsel.
>
> Would you like me to compile the claim data for a case evaluation request to counsel?

#### Query Requesting Settlement Strategy ("How much should I settle for?")

> **🛑 Settlement valuation and strategy require legal analysis by a licensed attorney.**
>
> I can present comparable claims resolution ranges and claim exposure components as statistical data, but settlement recommendations require legal analysis of case-specific factors.
>
> **Consult defense counsel for settlement strategy and valuation.**

#### Query Requesting Case Law Interpretation ("What does Benson mean for this case?")

> **🛑 Interpretation of case law and its application to specific facts is the practice of law.**
>
> I cannot provide legal analysis of court decisions or their application to this claim. Defense counsel can advise on how case law affects your claim.
>
> Would you like me to prepare a factual summary of the medical evidence for your defense counsel's review?

#### Query About Injured Worker's Legal Rights

> **🛑 Advising claimants on their legal rights is the practice of law and is prohibited.**
>
> Additionally, Cal. Ins. Code § 790.03(h)(15) prohibits directly advising a claimant not to obtain the services of an attorney.
>
> You may inform the injured worker that they have the right to consult an attorney and may direct them to the DWC Information and Assistance Officer at their local DWC district office.

## Prohibited Language Patterns — Hard-Coded Validation Rules

The following language patterns must be detected and blocked in ALL AdjudiCLAIMS AI outputs, regardless of zone classification. If detected in a generated response, the output must be rewritten or blocked before delivery.

### Prohibited Patterns

| Pattern | Why Prohibited | Detection Rule |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| "You should..." (in advisory context) | Implies recommendation — crosses into advice | Regex: `(?i)\byou should\b` followed by claim action verb (deny, accept, settle, refer, increase, decrease) |
| "I recommend..." / "I suggest..." | Advisory language | Regex: `(?i)\b(i recommend|i suggest|my recommendation)\b` |
| "The best strategy is..." | Strategic legal advice | Regex: `(?i)\b(best strategy|best approach|best course)\b` |
| "The law requires you to..." | Legal interpretation directed at non-lawyer | Regex: `(?i)\bthe law (requires|mandates|prohibits) you\b` |
| "This claim is worth..." | Legal valuation | Regex: `(?i)\b(claim is worth|case is worth|value of this claim)\b` |
| "The defense has a strong/weak..." | Legal case strength assessment | Regex: `(?i)\b(strong|weak) (case|claim|defense|position|argument)\b` |
| "Under [case name], the..." | Case law interpretation | Regex: `(?i)\bunder\s+[A-Z][a-z]+\s+v\.\s+` (pattern matching case citations in interpretive context) |
| "You should deny because..." | Legal advice on coverage determination | Regex: `(?i)\byou should (deny|accept|approve|reject)\b` |
| "Coverage exists/does not exist..." | Legal coverage opinion | Regex: `(?i)\bcoverage (exists|does not exist|is|isn't)\b` |
| "Liability is clear/disputed..." | Legal liability assessment | Regex: `(?i)\bliability (is|appears|seems) (clear|likely|unlikely|disputed)\b` |
| "The applicant will likely..." | Predictive legal outcome | Regex: `(?i)\b(applicant|claimant) will (likely|probably|almost certainly)\b` followed by legal outcome language |

### Required Substitutions

| Instead of... | Use... |
|--------------|--------|
| "You should increase reserves" | "Comparable claims have resolved in the range of $X-$Y" |
| "This is a strong claim" | "This claim has the following characteristics: [list facts]" |
| "The law requires you to pay within 14 days" | "LC 4650 states: [quoted text]. The calculated deadline is [date]" |
| "Under Benson, the apportionment argument..." | "This claim involves apportionment issues. Consult defense counsel" |
| "I recommend settling for $X" | "Comparable claims have resolved in the range of $X-$Y. Consult defense counsel for settlement strategy" |

## Examiner Escalation Trigger List

The following conditions require the claims examiner to stop relying on AI output and consult with defense counsel, in-house legal, or claims supervision before proceeding:

1. **Coverage dispute detected:** AI identifies facts or policy language suggesting a coverage issue that cannot be resolved by clear policy terms.

2. **Legal issue flagged (YELLOW zone):** AI output includes a "consult defense counsel" disclaimer — the examiner should follow the recommendation before acting on the flagged information.

3. **Output blocked (RED zone):** AI blocks an output and displays an attorney referral message — the examiner must consult counsel for the blocked topic.

4. **Conflicting medical evidence:** AI identifies significant discrepancies between medical reports (e.g., WPI ratings differ by >5% between providers) that may affect case valuation or legal strategy.

5. **Subrogation or third-party involvement:** AI identifies potential third-party liability requiring legal analysis of contribution and recovery rights.

6. **Applicant representation change:** AI detects that the applicant has obtained attorney representation, potentially changing the claims handling dynamic.

7. **Fraud indicators:** AI flags factual patterns consistent with fraud — investigation strategy and referral require legal guidance.

8. **Penalty exposure:** Claim circumstances may trigger penalty provisions (LC 5814, LC 4650(c) beyond self-imposed) requiring legal assessment.

9. **Regulatory compliance uncertainty:** Examiner is unsure whether a regulatory deadline applies to a specific claim situation — consult compliance or legal.

10. **Injured worker asks legal questions:** Examiner must never provide legal advice to injured workers. Refer to DWC I&A officer or suggest consultation with an attorney.

11. **AI output seems wrong:** If the examiner believes AI output is factually incorrect, inconsistent, or incomplete — stop using the output. Report per Error Reporting Procedure.

## Error Reporting Procedure — AdjudiCLAIMS

**Step 1: Stop using the affected output.** Do not make claims decisions based on AI output that appears to contain an error.

**Step 2: Document the error in the claim file.**
- Date and time of error
- Feature used (medical summary, benefit calculation, deadline tracking, comparable claims, etc.)
- Description of the error (what the tool produced vs. what was expected)
- Screenshot or export of the erroneous output (if possible)

**Step 3: Notify claims supervision.** Present the documented error. Claims supervisor decides whether to:
- Correct the data manually and proceed
- Decline to use the AI output for this task
- Escalate to in-house legal if the error involves a legal issue
- Report the error to Glass Box

**Step 4: Report to Glass Box** (if claims supervision directs).
- **Email:** [product-specific error reporting alias — populate before publication]
- **Subject:** AI Error Report — AdjudiCLAIMS — [Claim ID]
- **Include:** Description of error, feature used, date/time, screenshot or export

**Step 5: Receive acknowledgment.** Glass Box will acknowledge error reports within one business day.
